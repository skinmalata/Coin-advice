# Top 10 Lowest Fee Crypto Exchanges in 2026

Trading fees can eat into your profits faster than you might expect, especially if you trade frequently. A 0.5% fee on every trade means losing 5% of your portfolio annually if you trade just 10 times a month. That's why choosing a low-fee exchange is crucial for active traders.

This guide ranks the top 10 cryptocurrency exchanges with the lowest fees in 2026, covering both centralized exchanges (CEXs) and decentralized exchanges (DEXs). We'll break down maker/taker fees, withdrawal costs, and hidden charges to help you keep more of your hard-earned money.

## How Crypto Exchange Fees Work

Before diving into the rankings, let's understand the different types of fees you'll encounter:

- **Maker Fees**: Charged when you add liquidity to the order book (place a limit order that doesn't fill immediately)
- **Taker Fees**: Charged when you remove liquidity from the order book (place a market order or limit order that fills immediately)
- **Deposit Fees**: Most exchanges don't charge these for crypto deposits, but fiat deposits may incur fees
- **Withdrawal Fees**: Vary by cryptocurrency and network congestion
- **Spread**: The difference between buy and sell prices, often overlooked but can be significant

## How to Reduce Your Trading Fees

Beyond choosing a low-fee exchange, you can further reduce costs:

1. **Hold Native Tokens**: BNB, OKB, KCS, and other exchange tokens provide fee discounts.
2. **Increase Trading Volume**: Most exchanges offer VIP tiers with lower fees as your volume grows.
3. **Use Limit Orders**: Maker fees are often lower than taker fees.
4. **Batch Withdrawals**: Reduce withdrawal frequency to minimize network fees.
5. **Use DEX Aggregators**: 1inch and similar tools find the best prices across DEXs.
