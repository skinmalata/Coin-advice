How to Use Bybit's Trading Interface: Complete Tutorial

By Coin Advice | Updated: April 30, 2026

Bybit has built one of the most intuitive and powerful trading interfaces in the cryptocurrency space. Whether you're trading spot, futures, or options, mastering the platform is essential for executing your strategy effectively.

This comprehensive tutorial walks you through every aspect of Bybit's trading interface, from basic navigation to advanced features used by professional traders.

Accessing Bybit's Trading Interface

Log in to your Bybit account and click on "Trade" in the top navigation bar. You'll see several options:

  • Spot Trading: Buy and sell actual cryptocurrencies
  • Derivatives: Futures and perpetual contracts with leverage
  • Options: Trade crypto options (available on select coins)
  • Copy Trading: Follow and copy successful traders

For this tutorial, we'll focus on the Derivatives interface, as it contains the most features. The Spot interface is similar but with fewer order types.

Understanding the Trading Layout

Bybit's trading interface is divided into several key sections:

1. Market Selector (Top Left)

Choose which trading pair you want to trade. Bybit organizes markets into tabs:

  • Favorites: Your bookmarked pairs
  • USDT Perpetual: Most popular futures contracts
  • Inverse Perpetual: Contracts settled in the underlying coin (e.g., BTCUSD)
  • USDC Perpetual: USDC-settled contracts with lower fees
  • Options: Available options contracts

2. Chart Area (Center)

The largest section displays price charts powered by TradingView. This professional-grade charting tool includes:

  • Dozens of technical indicators (RSI, MACD, Bollinger Bands, etc.)
  • Drawing tools (trendlines, Fibonacci retracements, etc.)
  • Multiple timeframes (1m to 1M)
  • Real-time price updates

3. Order Book (Right Side)

Displays current buy (green) and sell (red) orders. Theorder book shows:

  • Price: The price level of each order
  • Size: The quantity being bought or sold
  • Total: Cumulative size up to that price level

Large orders near the current price create "walls" that can act as support or resistance.

4. Trade History (Below Order Book)

Shows recent trades executed on the platform, including:

  • Price: The execution price
  • Size: The quantity traded
  • Time: When the trade occurred

Rapid, large trades can indicate institutional activity or significant market events.

5. Order Entry Panel (Bottom)

This is where you place your trades. The panel includes:

  • Buy/Long: Betting the price will rise
  • Sell/Short: Betting the price will fall
  • Order Types: Market, Limit, Stop-Limit, etc.
  • Leverage Slider: Adjust your leverage (1x to 100x)
  • Order Value: Enter the amount you want to trade

Setting Up Your Charts

Proper chart setup is crucial for technical analysis. Here's how to configure Bybit's TradingView charts:

Choosing a Timeframe

Select a timeframe based on your trading style:

  • Scalping: 1m, 3m, 5m charts
  • Day Trading: 15m, 30m, 1h charts
  • Swing Trading: 4h, 1D charts
  • Position Trading: 1D, 1W charts

Adding Indicators

Click the "Indicators" button (fx symbol) at the top of the chart. Popular choices include:

  • RSI (Relative Strength Index): Identifies overbought/oversold conditions
  • MACD: Shows momentum and trend changes
  • Bollinger Bands: Measures volatility and potential reversal points
  • Moving Averages: Identifies trend direction

Drawing Tools

Access drawing tools by clicking the pencil icon. Essential tools include:

  • Trendlines: Connect swing highs or lows to identify trends
  • Horizontal Lines: Mark support and resistance levels
  • Fibonacci Retracement: Identify potential reversal zones
  • Rectangles: Highlight ranges or consolidation areas

Placing Different Order Types

Bybit offers several order types to execute various strategies:

Market Order

Executes immediately at the best available price. Use for quick entries/exits when speed matters more than precise pricing.

Steps:
  1. Select "Market" order type
  2. Choose "Buy/Long" or "Sell/Short"
  3. Enter the quantity (USDT or coins)
  4. Click "Buy/Sell" to execute
Warning: Market orders during low liquidity can suffer from slippage.

Limit Order

Places an order at a specific price or better. The order only fills if the market reaches your specified price.

Steps:
  1. Select "Limit" order type
  2. Enter your desired price
  3. Enter the quantity
  4. Click "Place Order"

Limit orders give you price control but may not fill if the market doesn't reach your price.

Stop-Limit Order

Combines a stop price (trigger) with a limit price (execution). Useful for entering breakouts or setting stop-losses.

Steps:
  1. Select "Stop-Limit" order type
  2. Set the stop price (trigger)
  3. Set the limit price (execution)
  4. Enter quantity and place order

Conditional Order

Places an order that activates only when certain conditions are met (last price, mark price, or index price reaching a specified level).

Using Leverage on Bybit

Bybit offers adjustable leverage from 1x to 100x (varies by coin). Here's how to use it:

  1. Locate the leverage slider in the order entry panel
  2. Drag to your desired leverage (or click to type a specific value)
  3. The interface shows your max position size based on your account balance and leverage
Risk Warning: Higher leverage increases both potential profits AND losses. Most professional traders use 2x-10x max.

Advanced Features

Isolated vs Cross Margin

  • Isolated Margin: Risk is limited to the margin allocated to that position
  • Cross Margin: Uses your entire account balance to avoid liquidation

Choose Isolated for individual position risk control, Cross for maximizing position size.

Trailing Stop

A dynamic stop-loss that moves in your favor as the price moves in your direction. If the price reverses by the specified amount, the stop triggers.

Take Profit/Stop Loss (TP/SL)

Set these when placing your order to automatically close positions at profit targets or loss limits.

Copy Trading on Bybit

Bybit's copy trading feature allows you to automatically replicate successful traders' positions.

How to Start Copy Trading:
  1. Navigate to "Copy Trading" in the main menu
  2. Browse the leaderboard of master traders
  3. Review their ROI, win rate, max drawdown, and trading style
  4. Click "Copy" and allocate funds
  5. Your account will automatically mirror their trades

Mobile Trading

Bybit's mobile app offers nearly all features available on desktop. Download it from the App Store or Google Play. Key mobile features:

  • Full trading functionality
  • Real-time price alerts
  • Biometric login (fingerprint/face recognition)
  • Copy trading management
  • Portfolio tracking

Security Tips for Bybit Trading

  1. Enable 2FA: Use an authenticator app, not SMS
  2. Whitelist withdrawals: Prevent unauthorized withdrawals
  3. Use strong passwords: Unique to your Bybit account
  4. Beware of phishing: Always type Bybit.com directly
  5. Withdraw to cold storage: Store large holdings in a Ledger hardware wallet
