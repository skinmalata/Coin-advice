Two platforms stand out for leverage trading: PrimeXBT with its massive 200x leverage and multi-asset offering, and Bybit with its trader-centric interface and rock-solid uptime.

This detailed comparison helps you decide which platform better suits your leverage trading needs.

Company Background

PrimeXBT

Founded in 2018 and registered in Seychelles, PrimeXBT built its reputation on high-leverage trading across multiple asset classes. The platform focuses on simplicity and privacy, allowing trading without mandatory KYC.

Bybit

Also founded in 2018 and headquartered in Dubai, Bybit has become the go-to platform for professional derivatives traders. With 99.99% uptime even during extreme volatility, Bybit has earned trust through reliability.

Leverage Comparison

PrimeXBT Leverage Offerings

  • Cryptocurrencies: Up to 200x (BTC, ETH), 100x (altcoins)
  • Forex: Up to 1000x on major pairs (EUR/USD, etc.)
  • Commodities: Up to 500x (gold, silver, oil)
  • Indices: Up to 100x (S&P 500, Nasdaq, etc.)
Warning: 200x leverage means a 0.5% price move against you wipes out your entire position. Extreme caution is required.

Bybit Leverage Offerings

  • Cryptocurrencies: Up to 100x on BTC, ETH; 50x on altcoins
  • Futures: Quarterly contracts with up to 100x leverage
  • Options: Limited leverage (varies by contract)
  • No Forex/Commodities: Crypto-focused platform
Winner: PrimeXBT for higher leverage and multi-asset options.

Risk Management for Leverage Trading

Regardless of platform, leverage trading requires strict risk management:

  1. Use Low Leverage: 2x-10x is plenty for most traders
  2. Always Use Stop-Loss: Never enter a trade without one
  3. Risk 1-2% Per Trade: Don't bet the house on one position
  4. Avoid Overleveraging: High leverage = rapid liquidation
  5. Take Profits: Set take-profit orders and stick to them

Final Verdict

PrimeXBT is better for multi-asset traders who want maximum leverage (200x) and no KYC requirements. The platform is simpler but offers less transparency and fewer cryptocurrencies. Bybit is better for crypto-focused traders who want a reliable platform (99.99% uptime), professional tools, and access to 600+ cryptocurrencies. The lower max leverage (100x) is still extremely high.

Neither platform serves US residents. For US traders, Binance.US or Kraken are better options.
