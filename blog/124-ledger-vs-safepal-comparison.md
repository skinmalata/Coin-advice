# Ledger vs SafePal: Hardware Wallet Comparison 2026

When shopping for a hardware wallet, the choice often comes down to Ledger versus Trezor. However, SafePal has emerged as a compelling alternative, especially for budget-conscious users who still want strong security.

In this comparison, we will pit the Ledger Nano X against the SafePal S1 to see how they stack up in security, usability, supported coins, and value for money.

## Price: SafePal Wins Easily

The most obvious difference is the price. SafePal S1 retails for roughly $49, while the Ledger Nano X is $149. That is a $100 premium for the Ledger.

If you are on a very tight budget, SafePal gives you hardware-level security at one-third the price. However, as we will see, you give up certain features and conveniences.

## Security Architecture

### Ledger Nano X
Uses a CC EAL5+ certified Secure Element chip, the same type found in credit cards and passports. This chip is designed to resist physical attacks, side-channel attacks, and glitching. The firmware is closed-source, which Ledger argues is necessary to protect their intellectual property.

### SafePal S1
Uses a general-purpose microcontroller without a secure element. However, SafePal has a unique "air-gapped" design—it has no USB or Bluetooth connectivity. All data transfer happens via QR codes, which eliminates an entire class of attack vectors (no USB malware, no Bluetooth exploits).

**Winner:** Ledger Nano X for physical attack resistance. However, SafePal's air-gapped design is actually more secure against remote attacks.

## Supported Cryptocurrencies

### Ledger Nano X
Supports 5,500+ cryptocurrencies including Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, Cardano, Polkadot, and virtually every ERC-20 and altcoin you can think of. The device can hold about 100 apps simultaneously.

### SafePal S1
Supports an impressive 30,000+ coins and tokens. SafePal has strong support for Binance Smart Chain (BSC), ERC-20, TRC-20, and many other blockchain standards. The device can store unlimited wallets since it uses QR-based account switching.

**Winner:** SafePal S1 for raw number of supported coins, though Ledger covers all the major ones most users need.

## Display and Usability

### Ledger Nano X
Has a 128x64 pixel monochrome display. It is small but functional, showing addresses, transaction details, and menu options. The two physical buttons require navigation by clicking through options.

### SafePal S1
Features a much larger 320x240 color LCD display. The screen is bright, colorful, and easier to read than Ledger's monochrome display. It also has a built-in camera for scanning QR codes.

**Winner:** SafePal S1 for the larger, color display.

## Connectivity: Wired/Bluetooth vs Air-Gapped

### Ledger Nano X
Supports USB-C connection to computers and Bluetooth to smartphones. The Bluetooth feature is excellent for managing crypto on the go through the Ledger Live mobile app.

### SafePal S1
Completely air-gapped. It has no USB port and no Bluetooth. All communication happens via QR codes displayed on your computer screen (which the SafePal camera scans) and QR codes displayed on the SafePal screen (which your phone/computer scans).

**Pros of Air-Gapped:**
- No USB malware can infect it
- No Bluetooth exploits
- Cannot be remotely hacked

**Cons of Air-Gapped:**
- More cumbersome to use (scanning QR codes back and forth)
- Requires a device with a camera (phone or webcam on computer)
- Slower transaction process

**Winner:** Subjective. Ledger is more convenient; SafePal is more secure against remote attacks.

## Software Experience

### Ledger Live (Ledger)
Polished, feature-rich desktop and mobile application. Supports sending/receiving, staking, buying crypto, swapping, and managing NFTs. Regular updates and new features.

### SafePal App (SafePal)
The SafePal mobile app is well-designed and supports the same features: portfolio tracking, sending/receiving, swapping, and staking. However, the experience is not as polished as Ledger Live, and the QR code workflow adds friction.

**Winner:** Ledger Nano X, due to Ledger Live's superior software and Bluetooth mobile experience.

## Build Quality and Design

### Ledger Nano X
Compact, credit-card-thick device with a premium feel. Stainless steel and plastic construction. Pocketable and durable.

### SafePal S1
Credit card-sized (thicker) with a sleek design. The device feels premium and includes a self-destruct mechanism—if someone tries to open the case, the secure element wipes itself.

**Winner:** Tie. Both feel premium, though SafePal's self-destruct feature is a nice security addition.

## Who Should Buy SafePal S1?

**Choose SafePal S1 if:**
- You are on a very tight budget ($49 vs $149)
- You want a completely air-gapped device (no USB/Bluetooth)
- You hold a diverse portfolio of altcoins (30,000+ supported)
- You do not mind the QR code workflow
- You like the larger, color touchscreen

## Who Should Buy Ledger Nano X?

**Choose Ledger Nano X if:**
- You want the best possible security (Secure Element)
- You want Bluetooth mobile connectivity for convenience
- You prefer the most polished software experience (Ledger Live)
- You are willing to pay a premium for top-tier features
- You hold a diverse portfolio (5,500+ coins supported)

## Final Verdict

The **Ledger Nano X** remains the better choice for most users. The Secure Element provides stronger protection against physical attacks, the Bluetooth feature is genuinely useful, and Ledger Live is the most polished hardware wallet software.

The **SafePal S1** is an excellent budget option. At $49, it offers air-gapped security, a large color display, and support for 30,000+ coins. If you are comfortable with the QR code workflow and want to save $100, SafePal is a solid choice.

**For most users:** Buy the **Ledger Nano S Plus** (~$79) instead of either. You get the same Secure Element as the Nano X, support for 5,500+ coins, and a much better price than the Nano X.
