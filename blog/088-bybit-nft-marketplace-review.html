Bybit NFT Marketplace Review 2026

By Coin Advice | Updated: April 30, 2026

NFTs (Non-Fungible Tokens) revolutionized digital ownership, creating markets for art, collectibles, gaming items, and virtual real estate. Bybit entered the NFT space with a marketplace that leverages their existing trading infrastructure.

This review examines whether Bybit NFT Marketplace is worth your time and money in 2026.

What is Bybit NFT Marketplace?

Launched in 2022, Bybit NFT Marketplace allows users to:

  • Buy NFTs using USDT from spot wallet
  • Sell NFTs you own or create
  • Create (Mint) NFTs directly on the platform
  • Trade across multiple blockchains (Ethereum, BNB Chain, etc.)

Unlike dedicated NFT platforms like OpenSea or Magic Eden, Bybit integrates NFT trading directly into their existing exchange ecosystem.

Supported Blockchains and NFT Collections

Bybit NFT supports multiple blockchains:

Ethereum (ETH)

  • Pros: Largest NFT ecosystem, blue-chip collections
  • Cons: High gas fees for minting/trading
  • Popular Collections: Bored Ape Yacht Club, CryptoPunks, Doodles

BNB Chain (BSC)

  • Pros: Low fees, fast transactions
  • Cons: Smaller NFT ecosystem than Ethereum
  • Popular Collections: Pancake Squad, Alien Worlds, Talecraft

Other Supported Chains

  • Polygon (MATIC): Low-fee alternative to Ethereum
  • Avalanche (AVAX): Fast, eco-friendly blockchain
  • Arbitrum: Layer 2 solution for Ethereum
Note: Supported chains may change. Check Bybit's website for the latest list.

How to Buy NFTs on Bybit

Step 1: Access Bybit NFT Marketplace

  1. Log in to your Bybit account
  2. Navigate to "NFT" in the top menu
  3. Browse featured collections or search for specific NFTs

Step 2: Browse and Research NFTs

Before buying:

  • Floor Price: Cheapest NFT in the collection
  • Volume: Trading activity (higher = more liquid)
  • Rarity: Rare traits = higher value
  • Utility: Does the NFT provide benefits (games, communities, etc.)?
  • Team: Is the project team doxxed and reputable?

Use our Coin Advice Token Checker philosophy—research thoroughly before buying.

Step 3: Place Your Bid or Buy Now

Buy Now (Fixed Price):
  1. Click on an NFT you want
  2. Click "Buy Now"
  3. Confirm the USDT amount (from your spot wallet)
  4. Complete the purchase
Place a Bid (Offer):
  1. Click "Make Offer"
  2. Enter your bid amount (must be = floor price)
  3. Set bid duration (1-30 days)
  4. Submit your bid

Step 4: View Your NFT

Once purchased:

  1. Go to "My NFT" > "Collected"
  2. Your NFT will appear there
  3. You can sell, transfer, or hold

How to Sell NFTs on Bybit

Step 1: List Your NFT

  1. Go to "My NFT" > "Collected"
  2. Select the NFT you want to sell
  3. Click "Sell"
  4. Choose "Fixed Price" or "Auction"

Step 2: Set Your Price

Fixed Price:
  • Set your asking price in USDT
  • NFT sells immediately when someone accepts
  • You can cancel anytime before sale
Auction:
  • Set starting price and duration
  • Buyers bid against each other
  • Highest bidder wins at auction end

Step 3: Pay the Fee and Complete Sale

  • Bybit charges a 1% platform fee on sales
  • Funds go to your spot wallet after sale
  • You can withdraw to your bank or use for trading

Creating (Minting) NFTs on Bybit

Step 1: Prepare Your Digital Asset

  • Image/Video/Audio: JPG, PNG, GIF, MP4, MP3 supported
  • File Size: Usually under 50MB
  • Metadata: Name, description, properties/traits

Step 2: Mint Your NFT

  1. Go to "NFT" > "Create"
  2. Upload your file
  3. Add name, description, and properties
  4. Choose the blockchain (Ethereum, BNB Chain, etc.)
  5. Pay the gas fee (varies by blockchain)
  6. Your NFT is now minted and listed
Warning: Gas fees on Ethereum can be $20-100+. Consider BNB Chain or Polygon for cheaper minting.

Fees and Costs

Action Fee
Buying NFTs None (pay only the NFT price)
Selling NFTs 1% platform fee
Minting NFTs Gas fee (varies by blockchain)
Transferring NFTs Gas fee (varies by blockchain)
Listing NFTs Free
Note: Gas fees go to the blockchain network, not Bybit.

Pros and Cons of Bybit NFT Marketplace

Pros

  • Integrated with Exchange: Use USDT from your spot wallet
  • Multi-Chain Support: Ethereum, BNB Chain, Polygon, etc.
  • Low Platform Fees: Only 1% on sales (OpenSea charges 2.5%)
  • Familiar Interface: Same design as Bybit's trading platform
  • Secure: Backed by Bybit's security infrastructure
  • Easy Onboarding: No separate wallet setup needed

Cons

  • Smaller NFT Selection: Fewer collections than OpenSea or Magic Eden
  • Less Community Features: No creator royalties enforcement (varies)
  • Not Available in All Regions: US users restricted
  • Less Advanced Features: Lacks some tools dedicated NFT platforms offer
  • Gas Fees: Ethereum minting still expensive

NFT Investment Risks

Extreme Volatility

  • NFT prices can crash 90%+ in days
  • Floor prices aren't guaranteed to hold
  • Many NFTs go to zero

Illiquidity

  • Some NFTs may not sell for months
  • You may need to deeply discount to exit
  • No guarantee of finding a buyer

Scams and Rug Pulls

  • Fake collections mimicking popular projects
  • Creators abandoning projects after mint
  • Smart contract vulnerabilities

Gas Fee Risk

  • Ethereum gas fees can exceed NFT value for cheap NFTs
  • Network congestion makes trading expensive

Security Tips for NFT Trading

  1. Enable 2FA: Use authenticator app on Bybit
  2. Verify Collections: Ensure you're buying the official collection
  3. Beware of Phishing: Always type Bybit.com directly
  4. Research Thoroughly: Check team, roadmap, community
  5. Store Valuable NFTs Securely: Transfer to Ledger or other hardware wallet

Tax Implications of NFTs

NFTs have complex tax treatments:

  • Buying: Not taxable (crypto-to-NFT exchange)
  • Selling at Profit: Capital gains tax
  • Selling at Loss: Capital loss (may offset gains)
  • Creating/Minting: Not taxable (just paying gas)
  • Royalties Received: Taxable as income

Who Should Use Bybit NFT Marketplace?

Ideal For:

  • Bybit users who want integrated NFT trading
  • Beginners who find dedicated NFT platforms confusing
  • Traders wanting USDT pricing (instead of ETH)
  • Multi-chain collectors (ETH, BNB Chain, Polygon)
  • Investors comfortable with 1% fees (lower than OpenSea)

Not Ideal For:

  • US residents (Bybit restricted)
  • Serious NFT collectors (OpenSea has more selection)
  • Solana NFT enthusiasts (use Magic Eden)
  • Pro NFT traders (Blur offers better tools)
  • Those seeking zero fees (Blur is cheaper)

Final Verdict

Bybit NFT Marketplace is a solid, beginner-friendly platform for NFT trading—especially if you're already a Bybit user. The integration with your existing USDT balance, multi-chain support, and 1% fees make it attractive.

However, the NFT selection is smaller than dedicated platforms like OpenSea, and pro traders may prefer Blur's advanced tools. For Bybit users wanting to dabble in NFTs without learning new platforms, Bybit NFT is a great starting point.

Remember: NFTs are extremely risky investments. Most will lose value over time. Only invest money you can afford to lose completely, and always research projects thoroughly.
