# Binance Futures vs Bybit Futures: Which is Better in 2026?

Futures trading has become one of the most popular ways to trade cryptocurrency, allowing traders to speculate on price movements with leverage. Two platforms dominate this space: Binance Futures and Bybit Futures. Both offer high liquidity, advanced tools, and competitive fees—but which one is right for you?

This detailed comparison breaks down every aspect of both platforms to help you decide.

## What is Futures Trading?

Futures contracts allow you to trade the future price of an asset without owning it directly. Key features include:

- **Leverage**: Control large positions with small margin (e.g., 100x leverage = $1,000 position with $10 margin)
- **Long/Short**: Profit from rising (long) or falling (short) prices
- **Perpetual Contracts**: No expiry date (most popular)
- **Quarterly Contracts**: Expire every 3 months at a set price

**Warning**: Futures trading carries extreme risk. 70-80% of retail futures traders lose money. Never use leverage you don't fully understand.

## Leverage and Margin

### Binance Futures
- **Max Leverage**: Up to 125x on select pairs (BTC, ETH)
- **Margin Modes**: Cross Margin and Isolated Margin
- **Multi-Asset Collateral**: Use BTC, ETH, BNB, and other assets as collateral
- **Auto-Deleveraging (ADL)**: Queue system for liquidations

### Bybit Futures
- **Max Leverage**: Up to 100x on select pairs

- **Partial Liquidation**: Only liquidates the minimum necessary

**Winner**: Binance for higher leverage (125x vs 100x), Bybit for the Unified Account system.
