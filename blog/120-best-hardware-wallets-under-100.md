Not everyone has $200+ to drop on a premium hardware wallet like the Ledger Nano X or Trezor Model T. The good news is that you do not need to spend a fortune to secure your cryptocurrency properly.

In 2026, there are several excellent hardware wallets available for under $100 that provide strong security for your digital assets. Whether you are a budget-conscious investor or just starting out, these devices will keep your crypto safe from hackers and malware.

## Why You Need a Hardware Wallet Even on a Budget

You might think, "I only have $500 in crypto, is a hardware wallet worth it?" The answer is a resounding yes.

1. **Exchange hacks happen:** Even in 2026, centralized exchanges remain targets.
2. **Phishing is rampant:** Software wallets on internet-connected devices are vulnerable.
3. **Peace of mind:** Knowing your crypto is offline is invaluable.
4. **It grows with you:** As your portfolio grows, your hardware wallet scales with it.

If you have more than $300-500 in cryptocurrency, a $79 hardware wallet pays for itself by protecting you from a single mistake or hack.

## 1. Ledger Nano S Plus - Best Overall Under $100

**Price:** ~$79

The Ledger Nano S Plus is the clear winner in the budget category. It offers the same CC EAL5+ secure element chip as the more expensive Nano X, identical security, and a large display.

**Pros:**
- Same security as $149 Nano X (CC EAL5+)
- Large 128x64 pixel display (much better than old Nano S)
- Supports 5,500+ cryptocurrencies
- Stores ~100 apps simultaneously
- Polished Ledger Live software
- Excellent build quality

**Cons:**
- No Bluetooth (wired USB-C only)
- No battery (must be plugged in)
- Closed-source firmware

**Best for:** Users who want top-tier security at the lowest possible price.

## 2. SafePal S1 - Best Air-Gapped Option

**Price:** ~$49

SafePal S1 is the most affordable hardware wallet on this list. It is completely air-gapped, meaning it uses QR codes for all data transfer—no USB or Bluetooth connection.

**Pros:**
- Very affordable ($49)
- Completely air-gapped (QR codes only)
- Self-destruct mechanism if tampered with
- Supports 30,000+ coins and tokens
- Built-in camera for scanning QR codes
- Backed by Binance (adds credibility)

**Cons:**
- Smaller screen than Ledger
- Less polished software experience
- Limited third-party integrations
- Some users may find QR process cumbersome

**Best for:** Users on a tight budget who want an air-gapped device.

## 3. KeepKey - Best Large Screen

**Price:** ~$49

KeepKey has been around for years and offers a unique large-format design. Recently acquired by ShapeShift, it has seen new development and features.

**Pros:**
- Large, clear 256x64 pixel display
- Sleek, distinctive design
- Simple, easy-to-understand interface
- Integration with ShapeShift for trading
- Affordable price point
- ShapeShift acquisition brought new life

**Cons:**
- Limited coin support (40+ compared to Ledger's 5,500+)
- Slower development cycle than competitors
- Larger form factor (less portable)
- No secure element chip

**Best for:** Beginners who want a simple, affordable wallet with a large screen.

## 4. Trezor Safe 3 - Best Open-Source Budget Option

**Price:** ~$79

The Trezor Safe 3 is the newest budget offering from SatoshiLabs. It features a secure element (a first for Trezor) while maintaining their open-source philosophy.

**Pros:**
- Secure element chip (new for Trezor)
- Fully open-source firmware
- Touchscreen interface
- Supports 1,000+ coins
- Shamir Backup (split seed phrase)
- Trezor Suite software is polished

**Cons:**
- More expensive than SafePal/KeepKey
- Smaller coin support than Ledger
- Touchscreen may be less durable than buttons

**Best for:** Privacy advocates who want open-source and a secure element on a budget.

## Setting Up Your Budget Hardware Wallet

Regardless of which device you choose, follow these universal security rules:

1. **Buy directly from the manufacturer** or authorized resellers only
2. **Verify the package is sealed** and untampered
3. **Generate a new seed phrase** on the device itself
4. **Write down your seed phrase** on the provided sheet (never digitize it)
5. **Store the seed phrase** in a secure, fireproof location
6. **Test with a small amount** before transferring large sums
