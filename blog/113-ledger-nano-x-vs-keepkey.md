# Ledger Nano X vs KeepKey: Hardware Wallet Comparison

When shopping for a hardware wallet, most buyers narrow their choices to Ledger or Trezor. However, KeepKey has been quietly improving under new ownership by ShapeShift, offering a compelling alternative at a budget-friendly price point.

In this comparison, we will pit the Ledger Nano X against the KeepKey to see how they stack up in security, usability, supported coins, and overall value.

## Price: KeepKey Wins Easily

The most obvious difference is the price. KeepKey retails for roughly $49, while the Ledger Nano X is $149. That is a $100 premium for the Ledger.

If you are on a tight budget, KeepKey gives you hardware-level security at a third of the price. However, as we will see, you give up quite a bit in terms of coin support and features.

## Security Architecture

### Ledger Nano X
Uses a CC EAL5+ certified Secure Element chip, the same type found in credit cards and passports. This chip is designed to resist physical attacks, side-channel attacks, and glitching. The firmware is closed-source, which Ledger argues is necessary to protect their intellectual property.

### KeepKey
Uses a general-purpose microcontroller without a secure element. The firmware is partially open-source, but the device lacks the physical attack resistance of a secure element. However, it does feature a "PIN protection" system and some physical hardening.

**Winner:** Ledger Nano X, due to the Secure Element providing stronger protection against physical attacks.

## Supported Cryptocurrencies

### Ledger Nano X
Supports 5,500+ cryptocurrencies including Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, Cardano, Polkadot, and virtually every ERC-20 and altcoin you can think of. The device can hold about 100 apps simultaneously.

### KeepKey
Supports around 40 cryptocurrencies natively. This includes major coins like Bitcoin, Ethereum, Litecoin, Dash, and a few others. It does not support the vast majority of altcoins and tokens.

**Winner:** Ledger Nano X, by a landslide. If you hold a diverse portfolio, KeepKey will not cut it.

## Display and Usability

### Ledger Nano X
Has a 128x64 pixel monochrome display. It is small but functional, showing addresses, transaction details, and menu options. The two physical buttons require navigation by clicking through options.

### KeepKey
Features a larger 256x64 pixel display, which makes it easier to read addresses and transaction details. The screen is wider but not as tall. Like Ledger, it uses two buttons for navigation.

**Winner:** KeepKey, for the larger, more readable screen.

## Connectivity and Mobile Use

### Ledger Nano X
Supports USB-C connection to computers and Bluetooth to smartphones. The Bluetooth feature is excellent for managing crypto on the go through the Ledger Live mobile app.

### KeepKey
USB-C only, no Bluetooth. Mobile support is limited to the ShapeShift mobile app, which has limited functionality compared to Ledger Live.

**Winner:** Ledger Nano X, thanks to Bluetooth and superior mobile experience.

## Software Experience

### Ledger Live (Ledger)
Polished, feature-rich desktop and mobile application. Supports sending/receiving, staking, buying crypto, swapping, and managing NFTs. Regular updates and new features.

### ShapeShift (KeepKey)
The KeepKey integrates primarily with the ShapeShift platform. While ShapeShift is a legitimate exchange, the software experience is not as polished or feature-rich as Ledger Live. You can also use KeepKey with Electrum (for Bitcoin) or MyEtherWallet (for Ethereum).

**Winner:** Ledger Nano X, due to Ledger Live's superior software.

## ShapeShift Integration

KeepKey's acquisition by ShapeShift means the device is tightly integrated with the ShapeShift exchange. This allows for easy trading and swapping of assets directly through the interface.

However, some users may be uncomfortable with this level of integration, preferring a more agnostic hardware wallet that is not tied to a specific exchange.

## Build Quality and Design

### Ledger Nano X
Compact, credit-card-thick device with a premium feel. Stainless steel and plastic construction. Pocketable and durable.

### KeepKey
Larger, flatter device that resembles a large USB stick. The rectangular design is sleek but less pocketable than the Nano X. The build quality is decent but feels less premium.

**Winner:** Ledger Nano X, for portability and premium feel.

## Who Should Buy KeepKey?

**Choose KeepKey if:**
- You are on a very tight budget ($49 vs $149)
- You only hold major cryptocurrencies (Bitcoin, Ethereum, Litecoin, etc.)
- You do not need mobile Bluetooth connectivity
- You are comfortable using ShapeShift as your primary interface
- You want a larger screen for reading addresses

## Who Should Buy Ledger Nano X?

**Choose Ledger Nano X if:**
- You want the best possible security (Secure Element)
- You hold a diverse portfolio of altcoins and tokens
- You want mobile Bluetooth connectivity
- You want the most polished software experience
- You are willing to pay a premium for top-tier features

## Final Verdict

The **Ledger Nano X** is the clear winner in this comparison. It offers superior security (Secure Element), support for 5,500+ coins, Bluetooth mobile connectivity, and a polished software experience. The $100 premium is justified for anyone with a meaningful crypto portfolio.

The **KeepKey** is a budget option that may work for Bitcoin and Ethereum maximalists who do not hold altcoins. At $49, it is affordable, but the limited coin support and lack of advanced features make it hard to recommend for most users in 2026.

If you want a budget hardware wallet, consider the **Ledger Nano S Plus** (~$79) instead. It has the same security as the Nano X, supports all 5,500+ coins, and is only $30 more than the KeepKey.
