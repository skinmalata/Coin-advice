Ledger Stax Review 2026: The New Touchscreen Wallet

By Coin Advice | Updated: April 30, 2026

The Ledger Stax represents a bold departure from everything Ledger has done before. Priced at $399, this device features a curved E-ink touchscreen, wireless charging, and a sleek credit-card-like form factor designed by Tony Fadell, the inventor of the iPod. But is this premium device worth the hefty price tag, or is it style over substance?

In this review, we examine every aspect of the Ledger Stax to help you decide if this is the right hardware wallet for your needs.

What is Ledger Stax?

Ledger Stax is a premium hardware wallet that breaks away from the traditional USB-stick design. It features a 3.2-inch curved E-ink touchscreen display, similar to what you would find on a Kindle. The device is roughly the size of a credit card but thicker, and it supports wireless Qi charging.

The Stax runs the same secure element chip (ST33, CC EAL5+) as the Nano X and Nano S Plus, so the core security remains identical. What you are paying for is the user experience and physical design.

Design and Build Quality

The Ledger Stax feels premium in hand. The curved E-ink display is a joy to use, offering excellent readability even in bright sunlight. The touchscreen interface is intuitive, allowing you to navigate menus, enter passphrases, and verify transactions with taps and swipes rather than physical buttons.

The device measures 85 x 54 x 7 mm, making it feel like a thick credit card. It fits comfortably in your pocket, though it is noticeably larger than the Nano X. The build quality is excellent, with a metal frame and glass front that screams premium.

One standout feature is the magnetic stacking. You can stack multiple Stax devices together magnetically, which is useful if you manage multiple wallets. The device also supports custom lock screens—you can display an NFT or image on the always-on E-ink display.

Security Architecture

Security-wise, the Stax is identical to other Ledger devices. It uses the same ST33 secure element chip with CC EAL5+ certification. Your private keys never leave this isolated environment, and all transaction signing happens within the secure element.

The device supports the same BOLOS operating system, so application isolation and security features are consistent with the Nano series. Ledger Live is the companion software, and the Stax works seamlessly with it.

The Ledger Recover controversy applies to the Stax as well. The device is technically capable of the same seed phrase extraction features, though the service remains optional and requires your explicit consent.

E-Ink Touchscreen Experience

The 3.2-inch curved E-ink display is the star of the show. Unlike LCD or OLED screens, E-ink is perfectly readable in direct sunlight and consumes almost no power when the image is static.

Navigating with touch is far more intuitive than pressing physical buttons. Entering passphrases, PINs, and verifying transaction details is faster and less error-prone. The large screen can display full addresses, QR codes, and more information at once.

However, some users may find the lack of physical buttons concerning. Buttons provide tactile confirmation that you have pressed them, whereas touchscreens can sometimes register accidental touches. Ledger has implemented safeguards, but purists may still prefer physical buttons.

Wireless Charging and Battery

The Ledger Stax supports Qi wireless charging, which is a first for hardware wallets. The battery lasts for several weeks on a single charge with typical use, thanks to the power-efficient E-ink display.

Wireless charging is convenient—you can top it up on any Qi charging pad, including those designed for smartphones. A USB-C port is also available for wired charging if needed.

Supported Cryptocurrencies

Like the Nano X, the Stax supports 5,500+ cryptocurrencies. You can install about 100 apps simultaneously, depending on their size. Managing apps is done through Ledger Live, and the process is identical to other Ledger devices.

Ledger Live Integration

The Stax works seamlessly with Ledger Live on desktop and mobile. The setup process is similar: install Ledger Live, connect your Stax, install apps, and create accounts. The touchscreen makes entering your PIN and navigating menus more pleasant than on button-based devices.

Staking, buying crypto, and managing NFTs all work exactly as they do on the Nano series. The only difference is the improved user interface on the device itself.

Pros and Cons

Pros

  • Gorgeous E-ink touchscreen with excellent readability
  • Wireless Qi charging for convenience
  • Sleek, premium design by iPod creator Tony Fadell
  • Same top-tier security as Nano series
  • Custom lock screens with NFT/ image support
  • Magnetic stacking for multiple devices
  • Long battery life thanks to E-ink

Cons

  • $399 price tag is steep for most users
  • Touchscreen lacks tactile feedback of physical buttons
  • Larger form factor than Nano X
  • Ledger Recover concerns still apply
  • Closed-source firmware like other Ledgers

Who Should Buy Ledger Stax?

Buy the Stax if:
  • You want the absolute best user experience
  • Price is no object ($399 is fine for you)
  • You love the credit card form factor
  • You want wireless charging convenience
  • You manage multiple wallets (magnetic stacking)
Skip the Stax if:
  • You are budget-conscious (get Nano S Plus)
  • You prefer physical buttons over touchscreen
  • You want the smallest possible device (get Nano X)
  • You do not like Ledger's closed-source approach

Using Stax with DeFi

Like other Ledger devices, the Stax works beautifully with MetaMask for DeFi interactions. Connect your Stax to MetaMask, and you can use Uniswap, Aave, and other DeFi protocols while keeping keys secure on your hardware wallet.

Where to Buy

Always buy directly from the official Ledger website to avoid tampered devices. The Stax is also available at select authorized resellers, but be extremely cautious about third-party sellers on Amazon or eBay.

Final Verdict

The Ledger Stax is a technological marvel that pushes hardware wallet design forward. The E-ink touchscreen is genuinely excellent, and the wireless charging is convenient. If you have a large crypto portfolio and want the best possible user experience, the Stax is worth the premium.

However, for most users, the Nano S Plus or Nano X offers identical security at a fraction of the price. Ask yourself: is a fancy screen worth an extra $250-320? If the answer is yes, the Ledger Stax will not disappoint.
