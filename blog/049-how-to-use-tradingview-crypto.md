## Setting Up TradingView (First 10 Minutes)

### Step 1: Create Account
1. Go to **[tradingview.com](https://www.tradingview.com)**
2. Click "Sign Up" (top right)
3. Use email or Google/Facebook login
4. Verify email

**Free vs. Pro:**

- **Pro ($14.95/month):** 4 charts, 20 alerts (recommended)

### Step 2: Find Your Crypto
1. Top search bar: Type "BTCUSD" (Bitcoin)
2. Choose the exchange:
   - **BTCUSD (Coinbase)** for Coinbase prices
   - **BTCUSDT (Binance)** for Binance prices
   - **BTCUSDT (Bybit)** for Bybit prices
3. Click the result → Chart opens

**Pro tip:** Use the exchange where you trade. If you use [Binance](https://www.binance.com), choose BTCUSDT (Binance).

### Step 3: Choose Your Chart Type
Top toolbar → "Chart Type" icon:
- **Candlestick** (MOST IMPORTANT — use this)
- Line (too simple)
- Bar (old-school, not needed)
- Heikin Ashi (advanced, ignore for now)

**Stick to candlesticks.** Everything else is noise.

### Step 4: Select Timeframe
Top toolbar → "1D" (daily) for beginners:
- **1m, 5m, 15m:** Day trading (too noisy for beginners)
- **1H, 4H:** Swing trading
- **1D (daily):** Best for beginners (THIS is where the money is)
- **1W (weekly):** Long-term investing

**Recommendation:** Start with 1D (daily). Master it before using lower timeframes.

## Adding Indicators (The Only 5 You Need)

Don't add 20 indicators. Your chart will look like a mess.

### Indicator 1: Moving Averages (MUST HAVE)

**How to add:**
1. Top toolbar → "Indicators" → Search "Moving Average"
2. Add **three** MAs:
   - **20 SMA** (blue, short-term)
   - **50 SMA** (orange, medium-term)
   - **200 SMA** (red, long-term — THE MOST IMPORTANT)

**How to use:**
- Price above 200 SMA = Bull market (buy dips)
- Price below 200 SMA = Bear market (sell rallies)

**Right-click each MA → Settings → Change color and length.**

### Indicator 2: RSI (Relative Strength Index)

**How to add:**
1. "Indicators" → Search "Relative Strength Index"
2. Default settings (14 periods) — don't change

**How to use:**
- RSI > 70 = Overbought (sell zone)
- RSI < 30 = Oversold (buy zone)

**Pro tip:** Add horizontal lines at 30, 50, and 70 (right-click RSI panel → "Add Horizontal Line").

### Indicator 3: Volume (Already There!)

Volume bars are at the bottom by default.

**How to use:**
- High volume + price move = REAL move
- Low volume + price move = fakeout

**Add "Volume MA"** (Indicators → "Volume MA"):
- Price above Volume MA = Healthy uptrend
- Price below Volume MA = Weak uptrend

### Indicator 4: MACD (Advanced)

**How to add:**
1. "Indicators" → Search "MACD"
2. Default settings — don't change

**How to use:**
- MACD crosses ABOVE signal = Bullish
- MACD crosses BELOW signal = Bearish

**Pro tip:** Wait for crossovers ABOVE/BELOW the zero line (see our [MACD Guide](link-to-post-45)).

### Indicator 5: Bollinger Bands (Advanced)

**How to add:**
1. "Indicators" → Search "Bollinger Bands"
2. Default settings (20, 2) — don't change

**How to use:**
- Price touches upper band = Overbought
- Price touches lower band = Oversold
- Bands squeeze = Big move coming

## Drawing Tools (The Secret Weapon)

### Tool 1: Horizontal Line (Support/Resistance)

**How to add:**
1. Left toolbar → "Crosshair" icon → "Horizontal Line"
2. Draw at major support/resistance levels

**How to use:**
- Price bounces off line = Strong level
- Price breaks line = Weak level (or breakout)

**Pro tip:** Extend lines to the left (right-click line → "Extend left").

### Tool 2: Trendline (Uptrends/Downtrends)

**How to add:**
1. Left toolbar → "Trendline" icon
2. Connect 2+ swing lows (uptrend) or 2+ swing highs (downtrend)

**How to use:**
- Price above trendline = Uptrend (buy dips)
- Price below trendline = Downtrend (sell rallies)

### Tool 3: Fibonacci Retracement

**How to add:**
1. Left toolbar → "Fib Retracement" icon
2. Click at swing LOW → Drag to swing HIGH (uptrend)
3. Key levels: 0.382, 0.5, 0.618 (Golden Pocket)

**How to use:**
- Wait for price to drop to 0.618 → BUY
- Wait for price to rally to 0.618 → SELL

## Saving Your Layout (Don't Skip This!)

You spent 30 minutes setting up the perfect chart. You close the tab. Next time you open TradingView... it's gone.

**How to save:**
1. Top right → "Save Chart Layout"
2. Name it: "My Crypto Template"
3. Next time → Top right → "Load Chart Layout" → Select yours

**Pro tip:** Save separate layouts for:
- **Daily Trading** (MAs, RSI, Volume)
- **Swing Trading** (Fibonacci, trendlines)
- **Scalping** (1-minute chart, more indicators)

### 1. Adding 20 Indicators
**The mistake:** "More indicators = better!"

**The fix:** Stick to 3-5 indicators MAX.

**The fix:** Use daily chart (1D) for analysis. Check 4-hour for entry.

### 4. Ignoring Alerts
**The mistake:** "I'll just watch the chart 24/7!"

**The reality:** You'll overtrade from boredom.

**The fix:** Set alerts. Close the app. Wait for the signal.
