How to Withdraw from Binance to Bank Account: Step-by-Step Guide

By Coin Advice | Updated: April 30, 2026

Cashing out your cryptocurrency profits is just as important as buying and trading. Whether you've made gains on Bitcoin, earned yield through Binance Earn, or received crypto as payment, eventually you'll want to move funds from Binance to your bank account.

This comprehensive guide walks you through every method available in 2026, from direct fiat withdrawals to P2P trading. We'll cover fees, processing times, and troubleshooting tips to ensure your withdrawal goes smoothly.

Understanding Your Withdrawal Options

Binance offers several ways to move funds to your bank account, and the best method depends on your location, currency, and urgency.

Direct Fiat Withdrawal

The simplest method—sell your crypto for fiat (USD, EUR, GBP, etc.) and withdraw directly to your linked bank account. Processing times vary by region and payment method.

P2P (Peer-to-Peer) Trading

Sell your crypto directly to other users through Binance P2P. You receive payment via your preferred method (bank transfer, PayPal, etc.) while Binance escrows the crypto to ensure safety.

Crypto-to-Fiat Gift Cards

Some regions support selling crypto for gift cards, which you can then use or sell for cash. This method is less common but useful in restricted jurisdictions.

Method 1: Direct Fiat Withdrawal (Recommended)

This is the most straightforward method for most users. Follow these steps carefully to avoid delays or lost funds.

Step 1: Convert Crypto to Fiat

Before withdrawing, you need to sell your cryptocurrency for fiat currency.

  1. Log in to your Binance account
  2. Navigate to "Wallet" > "Fiat and Spot"
  3. Find the cryptocurrency you want to sell (e.g., BTC, USDT, ETH)
  4. Click "Sell" and select the fiat currency you want (USD, EUR, etc.)
  5. Enter the amount and review the exchange rate
  6. Confirm the sale—funds will appear in your fiat wallet within seconds

Step 2: Link Your Bank Account

If you haven't already linked a bank account, do so now:

  1. Go to "Payment Methods" in your account settings
  2. Click "Add Payment Method"
  3. Select your currency and payment type (Bank Transfer, SEPA, SWIFT, etc.)
  4. Enter your bank details exactly as they appear on your account
  5. Complete any verification steps required (small deposit test, etc.)
Important: Ensure the bank account is in your name. Binance will reject withdrawals to accounts under different names due to anti-money laundering (AML) regulations.

Step 3: Initiate the Withdrawal

  1. Go to "Wallet" > "Fiat and Spot"
  2. Click "Withdraw" and select "Fiat"
  3. Choose your currency (USD, EUR, GBP, etc.)
  4. Select your linked bank account
  5. Enter the withdrawal amount
  6. Review the fee and processing time
  7. Complete 2FA verification (authenticator app or SMS)
  8. Submit your withdrawal request

Step 4: Wait for Processing

Processing times vary by region and payment method:

  • SEPA (Europe): 1-2 business days
  • Wire Transfer (US): 1-3 business days
  • Faster Payments (UK): Same day to 1 business day
  • SWIFT (International): 2-5 business days

You'll receive email notifications when your withdrawal is processed and when funds arrive in your bank account.

Method 2: P2P Trading (Alternative)

Binance P2P is excellent for users in regions with limited banking support or those who want faster settlement.

How P2P Works

  1. Navigate to "Trade" > "P2P Trading"
  2. Select "Sell" and choose your cryptocurrency
  3. Filter merchants by payment method, currency, and price
  4. Enter the amount you want to sell
  5. Binance will escrow your crypto during the transaction
  6. The buyer sends payment to your bank account/payment method
  7. Once you confirm receipt, Binance releases the crypto to the buyer

P2P Pros and Cons

Pros:
  • Often faster than direct withdrawal (minutes to hours)
  • More payment methods available
  • Can sometimes get better rates than market price
  • Available in regions where direct withdrawal isn't supported
Cons:
  • Requires manual coordination with buyers
  • Risk of payment disputes (though Binance mediation helps)
  • Bank transfers can still take time to clear
  • Some payment methods have limits or fees

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Withdrawal Rejected or Delayed

Possible Causes:
  • Bank account name doesn't match your Binance account
  • Insufficient trading volume or account restrictions
  • Bank rejecting crypto-related transfers
  • Withdrawal amount exceeds daily/monthly limits
Solutions:
  • Verify your bank account name matches your Binance KYC exactly
  • Contact Binance support with your withdrawal ID
  • Try a different withdrawal method (SEPA vs SWIFT)
  • Break large withdrawals into smaller amounts

Bank Rejecting the Transfer

Some banks flag or reject cryptocurrency-related transfers. If this happens:

  1. Call your bank and explain the transfer is legitimate
  2. Provide any documentation Binance can supply (proof of funds, etc.)
  3. Consider using a different bank that's more crypto-friendly
  4. Try P2P trading as an alternative

Withdrawal Not Showing in Bank Account

If your withdrawal status shows "Completed" but funds aren't in your account:

  1. Check the transaction ID on Binance and provide it to your bank
  2. Wait the full processing time (banks sometimes take extra days to credit)
  3. Check if your bank requires additional verification for large transfers
  4. Contact Binance support if more than 5 business days have passed

Tax Implications

Withdrawing from Binance to your bank account may trigger tax events:

  • Selling crypto for fiat: This is a taxable event in most jurisdictions
  • Converting one crypto to another: Also taxable in many countries
  • Simply moving fiat to your bank: Not taxable (just moving funds)

Keep detailed records of all transactions using tools like the Coin Advice Portfolio Tracker for easy tax reporting. You can also export transaction history directly from Binance.

Security Best Practices

  • Verify bank details carefully: A wrong digit can send funds to the wrong account
  • Use whitelisted withdrawal addresses: Enable withdrawal address whitelisting in security settings
  • Beware of phishing: Always access Binance by typing the URL directly
  • Enable 2FA: Use an authenticator app, not SMS, for maximum security
  • Small test withdrawal: For large amounts, send a small test first

For long-term storage of crypto you don't plan to sell immediately, consider transferring to a Ledger hardware wallet instead of leaving it on any exchange.

Using Your Funds Wisely

Once your funds reach your bank account, consider your next steps:

  • Reinvest in crypto: Use Dollar Cost Averaging (DCA) to buy back in gradually
  • Diversify: Consider traditional investments alongside crypto
  • Earn yield: Platforms like Nexo offer competitive interest on fiat deposits
  • Hold cash: Keep some liquidity for future opportunities

Final Tips

  1. Plan ahead: Withdrawals take time—don't wait until you urgently need the funds
  2. Watch fees: Large withdrawals amortize fixed fees better than small, frequent ones
  3. Keep records: Save all transaction IDs and bank statements for tax purposes
  4. Stay informed: Crypto regulations change—what works today may differ tomorrow
